# Reading Comprehension Answers: Technology and Society

**Student**: student-1, age 17, Upper-Secondary English class
**Test**: Students read a 400-word article about the effects of social media on teenagers and answered five comprehension questions.

---

## Original Questions and Student's Answers

**Q1. According to the article, what are two positive effects of social media on teenagers? (2 marks)**

Student-1 answer: The article says that social media helps teenagers to communicate with friends who live far away and also it can help them learn about different cultures and opinions from around the world.

**Q2. The author uses the phrase "a double-edged sword". What does this expression mean in the context of the article? (2 marks)**

Student-1 answer: This means that social media has both good and bad sides. It can be helpful but it can also cause problems for young people.

**Q3. Identify three concerns that researchers have raised about teenage social media use, as mentioned in the article. (3 marks)**

Student-1 answer: First, researchers say that spending too much time on social media can reduce the hours of sleep. Second, it can cause anxiety because teenagers compare themselves to others. Third, some teenagers become addicted and cannot stop checking their phones.

**Q4. The article states that "schools must adapt their approach." Explain what the author suggests schools should do and why. (3 marks)**

Student-1 answer: The author thinks schools should teach students how to use social media in a responsible way. They should include digital literacy in the curriculum. This is important because students need to know how to protect themselves online and understand what information is reliable.

**Q5. Do you agree with the author's conclusion that social media regulation is necessary for teenagers? Give reasons for your answer based on the text and your own experience. (4 marks)**

Student-1 answer: I partly agree with the author. It is true that social media can be dangerous for teenagers because of the problems with sleep and anxiety that the article mentions. However I think that complete regulation is difficult because teenagers will always find ways to access social media. In my experience, I think it is better to educate young people about the risks rather than trying to control everything. The article also mentions that social media has positive effects so we should not forget these benefits. I think a balanced approach with some rules but also education is the best solution.
