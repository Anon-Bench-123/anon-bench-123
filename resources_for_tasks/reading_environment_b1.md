# How One City Cut Its Carbon Emissions

Copenhagen, the capital of Denmark, wanted to become one of the greenest cities in the world. In 2009, the city government made a plan to reduce its carbon footprint by 2025.

The first step was to change how people travel. The city built more bicycle lanes and made public transport cheaper and more reliable. Today, more than 60 percent of people in Copenhagen ride a bicycle to work or school every day. This has led to a big reduction in emissions from cars and buses.

The second step was renewable energy. Copenhagen invested in large wind farms near the coast. These wind farms now produce enough electricity for thousands of homes. The city also installed solar panels on public buildings, including schools and hospitals.

Waste reduction was another important part of the plan. The city created a system where almost all household waste is recycled or turned into energy. Very little waste goes to landfill sites. Citizens learned to separate their waste into different categories, and the city provided special bins in every neighbourhood.

Copenhagen also worked to protect its local ecosystem. The city planted thousands of new trees and created green spaces in areas that were previously used for parking. These green areas help to clean the air, support biodiversity, and give residents places to relax.

The results have been impressive. Between 2009 and 2023, Copenhagen reduced its carbon emissions by more than 80 percent. Other cities around the world are now studying Copenhagen's plan to see what they can learn from it.
