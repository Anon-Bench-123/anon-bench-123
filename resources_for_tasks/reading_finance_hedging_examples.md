# Business Correspondence Samples - Hedging Language

**Level:** C1
**Context:** Professionals aged 25-40 in Abu Dhabi (international finance)
**Purpose:** Teaching hedging expressions in business communication
**Number of samples:** 4

## Sample 1: Email - Market Analysis Report

**From:** Sarah Thompson, Market Analyst
**To:** Executive Team
**Subject:** Q3 Market Trends Analysis

Dear Colleagues,

Please find attached our Q3 market analysis. Based on current indicators, **it appears that** the MENA region **may** experience moderate growth over the next quarter.

**It seems** consumer confidence is gradually improving, though **the evidence suggests** this trend **could be** fragile given ongoing geopolitical uncertainties. We **tentatively** predict a 3-5% increase in our sector, though this **should be treated with some caution**.

**There is some indication that** our competitors are preparing to enter the UAE market, which **might** impact our market share. However, **preliminary data suggests** our brand loyalty remains strong.

We **would recommend** monitoring these developments closely over the coming months.

Best regards,
Sarah Thompson

## Sample 3: Email - Declining a Proposal (Diplomatically)

**From:** Michael Chen, Investment Director
**To:** External Consultant
**Subject:** Re: Proposal for Strategic Review

Dear Mr. Al-Mansouri,

Thank you for your detailed proposal regarding the strategic review project. We appreciate the time and effort you invested in preparing this.

After careful consideration, **it appears that** the proposed timeline **may not** align perfectly with our current priorities. Additionally, **the budget seems** somewhat higher than we **had anticipated** for this phase of our operations.

**It's possible that** we **might** revisit this proposal in Q2 next year when our strategic priorities **may have** shifted. **Perhaps** we **could** explore a more focused, phased approach that **might** be more suitable for our immediate needs.

**We would be interested in** discussing alternative options that **could potentially** address our core requirements within a more modest framework.

**I hope** this doesn't cause any inconvenience, and we **would certainly welcome** the opportunity to work together in the future under more favorable circumstances.

Warm regards,
Michael Chen

## Sample 4: Email - Giving Critical Feedback (Softened)

**From:** Director of Operations
**To:** Department Manager
**Subject:** Project Timeline Review

Dear Fatima,

I wanted to touch base regarding the Al-Ain project timeline. **It seems** we **may be** facing some challenges in meeting the December deadline.

**From what I understand,** there **appear to be** some resource allocation issues that **could** be contributing to the delays. **It's possible that** additional team members **might** help accelerate progress.

**I was wondering if** we **could** schedule a meeting to discuss how we **might** address these challenges. **Perhaps** there are alternative approaches we **haven't fully explored**.

**I recognize that** the team has been working hard, and **it's clear that** external factors have played a role. **It would be helpful** if we **could** establish a revised timeline that **more accurately reflects** current realities.

**Would you be available** for a discussion this week?

Kind regards,
James
