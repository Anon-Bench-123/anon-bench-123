## Presentation Transcript

Good afternoon. Today I will present about the impact of climate change on agriculture in East Africa.

Climate change is becoming one of the most serious challenge that farmers in our region are facing. In recent years, we have seen many changes in weather patterns which are affecting crop production significantly.

First, I want to talk about rainfall changes. In Tanzania and other East African countries, the traditional rainy seasons are no longer predictable. Farmers used to know exactly when to plant their crops, but now the rains come late or sometimes they are too heavy. This create big problems for agriculture. For example, last year in some regions, the rain came three weeks late, so many farmers lost their entire maize harvest.

The second issue is temperature increase. Studies shows that temperatures in East Africa have risen by approximately 1.5 degrees in the last 30 years. This might sound small, but it has major effects on crops. Some traditional crops like coffee and tea are very sensitive to temperature, and they need specific conditions to grow well. When it becomes too hot, the quality decreases and farmers get less money for their products.

Drought is another serious problem. In northern Tanzania and Kenya, we are experiencing more frequent droughts. Between 2020 and 2022, there was severe drought which affected millions of people. Livestock died because there was no water and no grass, and food prices increased dramatically. Many families who depend on farming suffered greatly during this period.

These challenges are forcing farmers to adapt. Some farmers are switching to drought-resistant crops like sorghum and millet instead of maize. Others are investing in irrigation systems, though this is expensive and not all farmers can afford it. There are also some new farming techniques like conservation agriculture which helps to preserve soil moisture.

However, adaptation is difficult for smallholder farmers who have limited resources. They need support from government and international organizations to access better seeds, training, and financial assistance.

In conclusion, climate change is already having real impact on East African agriculture. If we don't take action now, the situation will become worse and food security will be threatened. We need both local adaptation strategies and global efforts to reduce carbon emissions.

Thank you. I'm ready to answer your questions.
