# Speaking Sample: Debate Transcript

**Student**: student-1, age 16, Upper-Secondary English class
**Task**: Participate in a class debate on the topic "Social media does more harm than good." Student-1 is arguing in favour of the motion.

---

## Transcript of Student's Contribution

Student-1: Thank you. I want to argue that social media does more harm than good, and I have three main reasons for this.

My first point is about mental health. There are many studies that show young people who use social media a lot have more problems with anxiety and depression. This is because they are always comparing their lifes with other people who seem to be more perfect. And also the cyberbullying is a very serious problem that is increasing because of social media.

My second reason is that social media is very addicted. The companies that make these apps, they design them to keep you scrolling and scrolling. Teenagers they spend many hours every day on their phones instead of doing homework or spending time with their families. I think this is very worrying for our generation.

Finally, social media spreads false information very quickly. We have seen this with, for example, the pandemic, when many peoples believed in fake news about vaccines that they readed on Facebook or Twitter. This can be dangerous for the society.

Some people may argue that social media helps us to stay connected with friends and family. I agree this is true, but I believe we can find other ways to stay connected that are more healthier, like meeting in person or calling by phone.

In conclusion, I strongly believe that social media causes more damage than benefits for our society.
