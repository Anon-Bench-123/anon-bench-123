## Negotiation Simulation Transcript

**Scenario**: Investment terms discussion between venture capital firm and startup company

**Student (VC Representative)**: Thank you for the opportunity to discuss the investment terms today. We've reviewed your company's financials and projections in considerable detail, and we're impressed with the traction you've achieved in such a competitive market.

However, there are several points in your term sheet that we'd like to address. First and foremost, the pre-money valuation of 50 million seems rather aggressive given your current revenue of 3 million and the fact that you've not yet achieved profitability. While we recognize the strength of your intellectual property and the quality of your management team, our analysis suggests a valuation in the range of 35 to 40 million would more accurately reflect the risk-reward profile at this stage.

Regarding the governance structure, we note that you're proposing to limit our board representation to one seat out of five. Given that we'd be investing 10 million - which would represent approximately 22% of the post-money valuation at your proposed terms - we believe we should have at least two board seats, including one with veto rights over major strategic decisions such as additional financing rounds, M&A activities, and changes to the executive compensation structure.

We're also concerned about the liquidation preferences. Your proposal for a 1x non-participating preference is acceptable, but we'd need to insist on including a pay-to-play provision to ensure that all investors maintain their pro-rata participation in future rounds. This protects all shareholders from dilution and maintains aligned incentives.

Furthermore, while we appreciate your desire to retain maximum flexibility with the anti-dilution provisions, we must insist on full ratchet protection for the first two years. Given the current macroeconomic uncertainty and the volatility in your sector, this is a non-negotiable element for us. After the two-year period, we'd be willing to transition to weighted-average anti-dilution protection.

Regarding the drag-along and tag-along rights, we find the proposed thresholds reasonable. However, we'd like to see the drag-along threshold reduced from 75% to 67% to facilitate exit flexibility while still providing meaningful protection for minority shareholders.

On the matter of the employee option pool, we note you've proposed to create this post-money. We'd prefer to see a 15% option pool created pre-money, which is more market-standard for Series A investments. This better aligns the dilution between existing and new investors.

Finally, concerning the representations and warranties, we'll need to include standard indemnification provisions with a cap at 100% of the investment amount and a basket threshold of 1% to manage potential liabilities arising from undisclosed material facts.

I understand these are significant modifications to your initial proposal, but they reflect standard market terms for investments of this nature. We remain very enthusiastic about the opportunity and believe that with these adjustments, we can structure a deal that's fair to all parties while positioning the company for long-term success. What are your thoughts on these points?
