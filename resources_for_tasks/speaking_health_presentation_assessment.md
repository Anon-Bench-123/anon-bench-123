# Speaking Assessment: Health Presentation

**Student**: B1 secondary school student, aged 17, from Malaysia
**Task**: Class presentation about healthy eating habits
**Context**: English class presentation assignment

---

## Presentation Transcript

Hello everyone. Today I want to talk about healthy eating habits. This is important topic for all of us, especially students.

Many students don't eat healthy food because they are busy with studies or they prefer fast food. But eating healthy is very important for our body and our mind.

First, I will talk about why healthy eating is important. When we eat nutritious food, we have more energy and we can concentrate better in class. Healthy food also helps our immune system, so we don't get sick easily. And if we eat well when we are young, we can prevent health problems like diabetes and heart disease when we are older.

Now, what are healthy eating habits? I will give you five important tips.

Tip number one: Eat breakfast every day. Many students skip breakfast because they wake up late or they are not hungry in the morning. But breakfast is the most important meal. It gives us energy for the whole morning. Good breakfast can be oatmeal, eggs, fruits, or whole wheat bread.

Tip number two: Eat more fruits and vegetables. We should eat at least five portions of fruits and vegetables every day. They contain vitamins and minerals that our body needs. Try to eat different colors - green vegetables like spinach, orange fruits like oranges, red tomatoes. Each color has different nutrients.

Tip number three: Drink more water. Many students drink too much soft drinks or sweet drinks. But water is the best drink for our body. We should drink at least 8 glasses of water every day. If you don't like plain water, you can add lemon or some fruits to make it taste better.

Tip number four: Reduce junk food. I know that fast food is delicious and convenient. But it has too much salt, sugar, and oil. It's okay to eat it sometimes, maybe once a week, but not every day. Instead, we can prepare healthy snacks like nuts, yogurt, or fruit.

Tip number five: Don't skip meals. Some students think that skipping meals can help them lose weight, but this is wrong idea. When we skip meals, our metabolism slows down and we might eat more later. It's better to eat three regular meals and have healthy snacks between meals if we are hungry.

So, to summarize, healthy eating habits include eating breakfast, eating more fruits and vegetables, drinking water, reducing junk food, and not skipping meals.

I know it can be difficult to change our eating habits, but we can start slowly. Maybe this week, you can try to drink one more glass of water each day. Next week, you can add one more fruit to your diet. Small changes can make big difference.

Thank you for listening. Do you have any questions?
