# Speaking Assessment: Video Describing Hobbies

**Student**: B1 teenager, aged 14-16
**Task**: Recorded video describing favorite hobbies
**Context**: Project work in Yangon, Myanmar

---

## Video Transcript

Hi everyone! Today I want to tell you about my favorite hobbies.

My first hobby is playing guitar. I start... I started learning guitar two years ago. At first, it was very difficult because my fingers hurt and I couldn't make the good sounds. But now I can play many songs. My favorite songs to play is... are English pop songs. I practice every day after school for about 30 minutes. Sometimes I play guitar with my friends and we make small concert for our families.

My second hobby is drawing. I really love to draw cartoon characters and animals. I use colored pencils and sometimes watercolors. Last month, I enter... I entered a drawing competition at my school and I win... I won third prize! I was very happy. My art teacher said my drawings are improving a lot. On weekends, I often go to the park to draw the nature and the people.

I also like reading books. My favorite books are adventure stories and fantasy novels. Right now I am reading a book about a boy who traveled to magical world. It's very exciting! Reading helps me to learn new English words too. I usually read before I go to sleep, maybe for 20 or 30 minutes.

These hobbies make me happy and help me to relax after studying. Guitar help... helps me to express my feelings, drawing makes me creative, and reading take... takes me to different worlds.

In the future, I want to get better at guitar and maybe join a band. I also want to learn digital drawing on computer. I think hobbies are very important because they make our life more interesting.

Thank you for watching my video!

**Issues identified**:

2. **Subject-verb agreement**: "favorite songs to play is/are", "helps" not "help", "takes" not "take"
3. **Lack of connecting words**: Limited use of discourse markers between ideas (however, moreover, additionally)

**Strengths**: Clear enthusiasm, good range of vocabulary for hobbies, appropriate past tense use (mostly), self-correction shows awareness
**Overall**: Good B1 performance with natural teenage communication style
