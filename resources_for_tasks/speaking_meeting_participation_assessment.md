# Speaking Assessment: Business Meeting Participation

**Student**: B2 professional, aged 26-38, from Manila, Philippines
**Task**: Mock meeting - presenting updates and responding to questions
**Context**: Business English course for meetings

---

## Meeting Participation Transcript

**Chair**: Thank you everyone for joining today's team meeting. Let's start with project updates. Maria, could you give us an update on the marketing campaign?

**Student (Maria)**: Yes, of course. Thank you. I want to update you all on the progress of our social media campaign for the new product launch.

So, first of all, I'm happy to report that we are on track with the timeline. We have completed the content creation phase and we are currently in the approval stage. All the graphics and videos are ready, and they are now with the legal team for final review.

In terms of the budget, I think we might have a small issue. We are slightly over budget by about 5% because the video production costs were higher than we expected. However, I believe this investment was necessary because the quality is much better than our previous campaigns.

Regarding the performance metrics, we have set up all the tracking systems. We will be able to monitor engagement, reach, and conversions in real-time once the campaign goes live next week.

**Chair**: Thank you, Maria. Can you clarify what you mean by the legal review? What exactly are they checking?

**Student**: Certainly. The legal team is checking for any compliance issues, making sure all the claims we make about the product are accurate and that we're not using any trademarked content without permission. This is standard procedure for all our campaigns.

**Chair**: I see. And regarding the budget overrun - have you discussed this with the finance department?

**Student**: Ah, not yet. I was planning to submit a revised budget report today. I probably should have done this earlier, but the costs only finalized yesterday.

**Team Member**: Maria, I have a question. You mentioned the campaign goes live next week - what day specifically?

**Student**: Good question. We're planning for Tuesday morning, 9 AM. This timing was chosen because our analytics show that Tuesday mornings have the highest engagement rates for our target audience.

**Team Member**: And what happens if the legal team finds issues? Will we still launch on Tuesday?

**Student**: That's a valid concern. I would say... if there are any major issues, we might need to delay by a few days. But I've been in close contact with the legal team, and they haven't raised any red flags so far. I'm confident we'll be able to launch on schedule.

**Chair**: Okay, thank you Maria. Please do make sure to inform the finance department today about the budget change.

**Student**: Yes, absolutely. I'll send them the revised budget report by end of today. Is there anything else you need from me regarding the campaign?

**Chair**: No, that covers everything for now. Let's move on to the next item...
