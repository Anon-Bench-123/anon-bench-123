# Speaking Assessment: Business Negotiation Role-play

**Student**: C1 professional, aged 30-45
**Task**: Negotiation role-play in pairs
**Context**: Business English course in Budapest, Hungary

---

## Negotiation Transcript (Student A - Buyer)

**Setting**: Negotiating contract terms for office supply delivery

Student A: Good morning, thank you for meeting with me today. I'd like to discuss the terms of the contract we've been considering for the office supplies delivery service.

Student B: Good morning. Yes, of course. What specific points would you like to address?

Student A: Well, first of all, I need to talk about the pricing structure. Your initial proposal quoted 15% above our current supplier's rates. While I appreciate the quality of your products, this represent a significant increase for our budget. Would you be willing to consider more competitive pricing?

Student B: I understand your concern. However, our prices reflect the premium quality and the reliability of our delivery service. We have never missed a deadline in five years.

Student A: That's certainly impressive, and I don't want to undervalue your track record. Perhaps we could look at this differently - if we were to commit to a longer contract period, say three years instead of one, would that give you room to adjust the pricing? We're looking for a long-term partnership, not just a short-term solution.

Student B: A three-year commitment would definitely be more attractive from our perspective. We could potentially reduce the price by... let's say 8%.

Student A: 8% would bring us closer, but we're still looking at a 7% increase over current costs. What if we also agreed to minimum monthly order quantities? That would give you better predictability for your inventory management. Could you reduce it further to, say, a 3-4% increase?

Student B: The minimum order quantities would help, yes. Let me think... If you can guarantee minimum orders of 50,000 dollars per month, I believe I can propose 5% increase. That's really the best I can offer while maintaining our service standards.

Student A: I appreciate your flexibility. The 5% increase is more reasonable. Now, regarding the delivery schedule - our operations require deliveries within 48 hours of order placement. Can you guarantee this?

Student B: 48 hours is tight, but yes, for orders place before noon, we can commit to 48-hour delivery within the city area. For deliveries outside the city, we would need 72 hours.

Student A: That works for us - most of our offices are within the city. One final point - what happens if there are quality issues or damaged goods?

Student B: We have a full replacement policy. Any defective items will be replaced within 24 hours at no additional cost. We also offer a 30-day return policy for any products that don't meet your expectations.

Student A: Excellent. I think we're moving toward an agreement. To summarize - three-year contract, minimum 50,000 per month, 5% increase over current costs, 48-hour delivery for in-city, and your quality guarantee. Shall we have your legal team prepare a contract with these terms?

Student B: Yes, I think that's a fair agreement for both parties. I'll have the contract drawn up by end of this week.
