# Speaking Assessment Transcript

**Student**: Jihye, age 17
**Task**: Individual presentation (4-5 minutes) on the topic "A global issue that young people should care about"
**Context**: End-of-semester summative speaking assessment, B2 class

## Transcript

Good morning everyone. Today I want to talk about a topic that I think is very important for young people -- the problem of fast fashion and how it is affecting our environment.

First, I want to explain what is fast fashion. Fast fashion is when companies make cheap clothes very quickly and sell them for low prices. Brands like Zara, H&M, and Shein are examples. The problem is that because clothes are so cheap, people buy too many and throw them away very fast. In fact, the average person now buys 60 percent more clothes than 15 years ago but keeps them for only half as long.

So why should young people care about this? Well, there are three main reasons that I want to discuss.

The first reason is environmental. The fashion industry is responsible for about 10 percent of global carbon emissions. That is more than all international flights and shipping combined. Also, making clothes uses enormous amounts of water. For example, to produce one cotton T-shirt requires approximately 2,700 litres of water. And when people throw away their clothes, most of them end up in the landfills where they can take more than 200 years to decompose.

The second reason is about human rights. Many fast fashion brands manufacture their clothes in countries like Bangladesh, Vietnam, and Cambodia where workers are paid very low wages and work in dangerous conditions. In 2013, the Rana Plaza factory in Bangladesh collapsed and more than 1,100 people were died. Most of them were young women. I think as consumers we have a responsibility to think about who makes our clothes and under what conditions.

The third reason is psychological. Social media, especially Instagram and TikTok, creates pressure to always wear new outfits. There is even a term for this -- it is called "haul culture" where influencers show all the new clothes they have bought. This can make young people feel that they need to constantly buy new things to be accepted. I think this is damaging to our mental health and our sense of what is truly valuable.

So what can we do? I believe there are several things. First, we can buy second-hand clothes. In Korea, there are now many thrift shops, especially in areas like Hongdae and Itaewon. Second, we can take better care of the clothes we already have instead of throwing them away. Third, we can support brands that are committed to sustainable practices, even if they are more expensive. And finally, we can use our voice on social media to raise awareness rather than promoting overconsumption.

In conclusion, fast fashion is not just a fashion problem -- it is an environmental crisis, a human rights issue, and a challenge to our values. I believe that our generation has the power to change this, but only if we start making more conscious choices about what we wear.

Thank you for listening. Do you have any questions?
