# Speaking Sample: Oral Presentation Transcript (Excerpt)

**Student**: student-1, age 17, Upper-Secondary
**Task**: Give a 3-4 minute presentation to the class arguing either for or against the statement: "Mass tourism does more harm than good to local communities." (3-minute excerpt)

---

## Student's Transcript

Good morning everyone. Today I would like to talk about mass tourism and why I believe it is actually harmful for local communities. I hope you will find my presentation interesting.

First of all, I want to explain about the economic problems. Many people think tourism brings money to local people, but actually most of the money goes to big international hotels and travel companies. The local businesses, for example small restaurants and shops, they receive only a small part. According to a study I have read, in some countries only 20 percent of tourism money stays in the local economy. This is a problem that need to be solved.

My second point is about the environment. When thousands of tourists visit a place every year, they produce a lot of waste and pollution. For example, in my country, Thailand, the island of Koh Phi Phi was so damaged by tourists that the government had to close the beach for several years. The coral reefs was destroyed and the sea was full of rubbish. I think this shows clearly that mass tourism can to destroy natural environments.

Also, I would like to mention about the cultural effects. When a place becomes very touristic, the local culture often changes to please the tourists. Traditional festivals become performances for visitors, and local people start to sell souvenirs instead of practising their traditional works. Some people call this the "Disneyfication" of culture, which I think is a very good description.

To summarise, I believe that mass tourism does more harm than good because it does not help the local economy as much as people think, it damages the environment, and it changes local cultures in negative ways. However, I am not saying that all tourism is bad. What we need is a more sustainable approach to tourism that truly benefits the local communities.

Thank you for listening. I am ready to answer any questions.
