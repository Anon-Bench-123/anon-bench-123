# Pronunciation Assessment Recordings - A2 Adult Learners (Osaka, Japan)

## Context
**Course:** English Pronunciation for Adult Learners
**Level:** A2 (CEFR)
**Learners:** 10 Japanese adult learners in Osaka (5 featured for assessment focus)
**Focus:** Improving clarity in spoken English
**Recording Date:** Week 8 of 20-week course

---

## Recording Setup

**Task:** Students recorded themselves reading a short passage and answering questions
**Duration:** 3-4 minutes per student
**Purpose:** Identify priority pronunciation issues for feedback

---

## Audio Transcription Notes

### Student 1: Tanaka Yuki (Age 68, Female, Retired Teacher)

**Reading Performance:**
"I went to **Rondon** [/r/ → /l/] rast [/l/ → /r/] summer. It was a wonderfur [/l/ → /r/] trip. I bisited [/v/ → /b/] the British Museum and saw many interesting **singsu** [/θ/ → /s/, final consonant added]."

**Question Response:**
"I rike [/l/ → /r/] to read books and risten [/l/ → /r/] to music. Sometimes I pray [/l/ → /r/] piano."

**Pronunciation Issues:** /l/ and /r/ confusion (systematic); /v/ → /b/; /θ/ → /s/; final consonant clusters simplified

**Intelligibility Rating:** 3/5 (requires listener effort)



---

### Student 2: Yamamoto Hiroshi (Age 58, Male, Company Manager)

**Reading Performance:**
"I went to **Rondon** rast **sama** [summer]. It was **wan-da-fu-ru** trip. I **biji-ted** [visited] the **Buriti-shu** Museum."

[Heavy syllable-timing; words broken into mora-like units]

**Question Response:**
"I **raiku** [like] to **ri-do** [read] book and **pu-re** [play] **go-ru-fu** [golf]."

**Pronunciation Issues:**
1. **Syllable-timed rhythm instead of stress-timed** (every syllable given equal weight)
2. **/l/ → /r/** confusion systematic
3. **Extensive vowel insertion**
4. **Sentence intonation flat**

**Intelligibility Rating:** 2.5/5 (difficult to understand; choppy rhythm)



---

### Student 3: Kobayashi Yuko (Age 29, Female, Hotel Receptionist)

**Reading Performance:**
"I went to London last summer. It was a wonderful trip. I visited the British Museum and saw many interesting **シングス** [things]."

**Pronunciation Issues:**
1. **/θ/ → /s/** (only remaining systematic error)
2. Some vowel quality issues

**Intelligibility Rating:** 4.5/5 (very clear; minimal L1 interference)



---

### Student 4: Suzuki Akiko (Age 47, Female, Administrative Staff)

**Reading Performance:**
"I went to London rast summer. It was a wonderfur trip. I **bizited** [visited] the **Buritisshu** [British] Museum and saw many **interesutingu** [interesting] **sings** [things]."

**Pronunciation Issues:** /l/ → /r/ confusion; /v/ → /b/; vowel insertion (epenthesis) between consonants; /θ/ → /s/; frequent hesitations

**Intelligibility Rating:** 3/5



---

### Student 5: Ishikawa Midori (Age 38, Female, English Tutor for Children)

**Reading Performance:**
"I went to London last summer. It was a wonderful trip. I visited the British Museum and saw many interesting **sings** [things]."

**Pronunciation Issues:**
1. **/θ/ → /s/** (only systematic error)
2. Generally excellent pronunciation with good sentence stress and intonation
3. **/l/ and /r/ well-controlled**

**Intelligibility Rating:** 4.8/5 (nearly native-like)
