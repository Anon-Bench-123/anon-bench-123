# Speaking Assessment: Tour Guide Practice

**Student**: C1 learner, aged 25, from Costa Rica, tourism worker
**Task**: 15-minute recorded guided tour of coffee farm
**Context**: Practicing English for giving guided tours

---

## Tour Guide Recording Transcript (Key sections)

### Opening (Minutes 1-2)

Good morning everyone, and welcome to Finca Verde, our family's coffee farm here in the Central Valley of Costa Rica. My name is Carlos, and I'll be your guide today. We're going to spend about an hour together learning about how coffee is grown, processed, and finally becomes the delicious cup of coffee you enjoy each morning.

Before we start, I want to mention that you're welcome to ask questions at any time during the tour. Also, please watch your step as we walk - some paths can be a bit slippery after the morning rain.

Now, let me tell you a little bit about the history of this farm. My great-grandfather established this farm in 1952, so we've been growing coffee here for over 70 years now...

### Middle Section (Minutes 7-9)

...So now we're standing in one of our main growing areas. As you can see, the coffee plants are organized in rows under these taller shade trees. Can anyone guess why we grow coffee under shade instead of in full sun?

(Pause for responses)

Exactly! The shade trees protect the coffee plants from too much direct sunlight, which can stress the plants and affect the flavor of the beans. These shade trees also provide habitat for birds and other wildlife, which helps maintain the biodiversity of the area. In fact, you might notice some birds around us - they help control insects naturally, so we don't need to use as much pesticide.

The coffee plant takes about three to four years to produce its first harvest. During that time, we have to be patient and take good care of the plants - making sure they have enough water, nutrients, and protection from disease...

Now, if you look closely at this plant here, you can see these small white flowers. These flowers will eventually become the coffee cherries. The cherries start out green, then they turn yellow, and finally they become this deep red color when they're ready to harvest. We only pick the red ones because that's when they have the perfect level of sweetness and flavor...

### Closing (Minutes 14-15)

...And that brings us to the end of our tour. I hope you've enjoyed learning about the coffee-making process, from the plant to your cup. If you have any more questions, I'm happy to answer them now.

Before you go, please stop by our shop where you can purchase some of our farm's coffee. We also have some traditional Costa Rican snacks if you'd like to try. Thank you so much for visiting Finca Verde today, and enjoy the rest of your time in Costa Rica!
