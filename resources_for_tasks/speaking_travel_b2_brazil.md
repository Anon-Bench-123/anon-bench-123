# Speaking Assessment Transcript

**Student**: Renata, age 32
**Task**: Describe a memorable travel experience and explain what you learned from it (3-4 minutes)
**Context**: End-of-term summative speaking test, B2 class

## Transcript

OK so I will talk about my trip to Patagonia in Argentina. I went there in 2023 with my husband and it was... how can I say... one of the most incredible experiences of my life.

We flew to Buenos Aires first and then we took another flight to El Calafate. When we arrived it was very cold, much more cold than we expected. We didn't brought enough warm clothes so we had to buy some jackets there. It was funny because the shops was very expensive.

The main reason we went there was to see the Perito Moreno glacier. When I saw it for the first time I couldn't believe how big it was. It's like... you see it in the photos but when you are in front of it, it's completely different. You can hear the ice... breaking? Yes, breaking. Big pieces of ice fall into the water and the sound is like thunder. I was so emotional that I almost cried.

We also did a trekking on the glacier. It was very challenging because we had to wear special shoes -- crampons, I think they are called -- and walking on the ice is not easy. But the guide was very patient and he explained everything very well. At one point we drinked water directly from the glacier and it was the most pure water I ever tasted.

Another day we drove to Torres del Paine which is actually in Chile. The landscapes there are... I don't have words to describe. Mountains, lakes, animals... we saw guanacos and even a condor flying. My husband took amazing photos.

What I learned from this trip is that nature can make you feel very small but in a good way. Sometimes in the city we think our problems are very big but when you see a glacier that has been there for thousands of years, you understand that we are just a small part of something much bigger. Also, I learned that I am more brave than I thought because the trekking was really scary sometimes and I didn't give up.

I would definitely recommend this trip to anyone who likes adventure and... how do you say... being in contact with the nature. It changed something in me, I think.
