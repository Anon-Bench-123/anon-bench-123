# Speaking Sample: Discussion Transcript

**Student**: student-1, Adult learner, General English course
**Task**: Discuss the advantages and disadvantages of travelling alone versus travelling with friends. Give your own preference and explain why.

---

## Transcript of Student's Response

Well, I think this is a very interesting topic because I have experience of both types of travelling, so I can compare them quite well.

Let me start with travelling alone. The main advantage is the freedom. When you travel by yourself, you can decide everything -- where to go, what to eat, how long to stay in one place. Nobody is complaining that they want to do something different. For example, last year I went to Portugal alone for ten days and I could spend three days just in one small village near the coast because I loved the atmosphere there. If I was travelling with my friends, probably they would become bored after one day and want to move on.

But there is also disadvantages. The biggest one is that sometimes you feel a bit lonely, especially in the evenings when you are eating dinner alone in a restaurant. And also, if something goes wrong -- like you lose your passport or you get sick -- there is nobody to help you. When I was in Portugal, actually my phone was stolen and I had to go to the police alone and it was really stressful because I didn't spoke Portuguese at all.

On the other hand, travelling with friends has many positive aspects. You share the experiences together, and often the funniest moments are the unexpected things that happens. The costs are also more cheap because you can share the hotel room and the taxi. And you feel more safe, especially if you are a woman travelling in a place you don't know well.

The disadvantage of travelling with friends is the conflicts. You have to compromise about everything. My friend likes to wake up early and visit every museum, but I prefer to be more relaxed. This kind of differences can create tension during the trip.

For me personally, I prefer to travelling alone because the freedom is more important for me than the company. But I think it depends very much on the type of trip and the destination. For a beach holiday, I like to go with friends, but for a cultural trip where I want to explore at my own rhythm, I choose to go alone.

So in general, both options has their merits and I think everyone should try both at least once.
