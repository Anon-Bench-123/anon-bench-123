# Lesson Observation Notes

**Teacher:** Ms B.
**Date:** 14 January 2026
**Class:** Upper-secondary, mixed-ability (12 students, B1-B2 range)
**Lesson:** Reading - Travel Blogs (45 minutes)
**Observer:** Senior Teacher

---

## Observation

**0:00-0:05 Lead-in**
Ms B. projected a photo of a popular beach in Thailand and asked students to talk in pairs about their last holiday. Most pairs spoke briefly in L1 before switching to English. Ms B. did not monitor the pairs and stayed at the front desk preparing materials.

**0:05-0:12 Pre-reading**
Ms B. handed out the reading text and a vocabulary list of 15 words with definitions. She read through each word and definition aloud, asking students to repeat after her. Two stronger students appeared disengaged during this stage, one resting their head on the desk.

**0:12-0:30 Reading task**
Students read a 400-word travel blog about backpacking in Vietnam. The task was to answer eight comprehension questions (all wh-questions requiring full-sentence answers). Ms B. told students to work individually and raise their hand if they had questions. Three students raised hands at different times; Ms B. answered each by pointing to the specific paragraph where the answer could be found. After 18 minutes, she asked students to compare answers in pairs but only gave them two minutes before moving to whole-class feedback.

**0:30-0:40 Feedback**
Ms B. nominated students to read their answers aloud. She confirmed correct answers by saying "yes" and for incorrect answers said "no, try again." One student gave an answer that was partially correct but Ms B. said "not quite" and moved to another student without explaining what was missing. There was no discussion of the text content beyond the literal comprehension questions.

**0:40-0:45 Follow-up**
Ms B. asked the class to write five sentences about a place they would like to visit, using vocabulary from the blog. Students had only started writing when the lesson ended. Ms B. told them to finish for homework.

---

**General notes:** Students were generally compliant but passive throughout the lesson. The classroom was quiet and orderly. Ms B. appeared well-prepared with materials printed and ready.
