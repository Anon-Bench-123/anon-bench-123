# Lesson Observation Notes

**Teacher:** Mr C.
**Date:** 21 January 2026
**Class:** 14 adult learners, B1 level
**Lesson:** Speaking - Future Plans and Ambitions (60 minutes)
**Observer:** Teacher Trainer

---

## Observation

**0:00-0:08 Warmer**
Mr C. began by telling the class about his own plans for next year, including studying for a masters degree and travelling to Patagonia. He spoke for about four minutes, using a range of future forms naturally. He then asked students to discuss in pairs what their plans were for the next twelve months. Pairs were engaged and spoke for about three minutes.

**0:08-0:20 Language focus**
Mr C. wrote three example sentences on the board from his own talk: "I'm going to study," "I'll probably travel," and "I'm starting my course in March." He asked students to identify the differences. Several students offered explanations. Mr C. clarified the distinction between plans (going to), predictions (will), and fixed arrangements (present continuous). He concept-checked with the whole class using two or three questions per form.

**0:20-0:35 Controlled practice**
Students completed a gap-fill exercise choosing the correct future form in context. Mr C. monitored actively, crouching beside desks and asking guiding questions when students were unsure. During feedback he asked students to justify their choices, which generated useful discussion. However, this stage overran by about five minutes.

**0:35-0:50 Speaking activity**
Mr C. set up a mingle activity where students interviewed three classmates about their future plans, noting key information. The instructions were given orally without any visual support. Two students appeared confused about what to do and began speaking to each other in Spanish. Mr C. noticed after about two minutes and re-explained to them individually. The activity ran well once established, with most students using the target language. Mr C. monitored but did not take notes on student language.

**0:50-0:58 Report back**
Mr C. asked three students to tell the class one interesting thing they learned about a classmate. The reports were brief and the activity felt rushed. There was no language feedback or error correction based on what students had produced during the speaking activity.

**0:58-1:00 Round-up**
Mr C. summarised the lesson briefly and set homework to write a short paragraph about their plans. The lesson ended on time.

---

**General notes:** Good rapport with students, relaxed and encouraging classroom atmosphere. Students appeared comfortable participating. Mr C. demonstrated confident command of the language.
