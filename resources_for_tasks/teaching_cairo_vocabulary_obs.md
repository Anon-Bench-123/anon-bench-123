# Lesson Observation Notes

**Teacher:** Ms D.
**Date:** 28 January 2026
**Class:** 18 adult learners (young professionals), A2 level
**Lesson:** General English - Vocabulary: Food and Restaurants (90 minutes)
**Observer:** Academic Coordinator

---

## Observation

**0:00-0:10 Lead-in**
Ms D. showed six photos of different types of restaurants (fast food, fine dining, street food, cafe, food court, buffet) on the projector. Students discussed in pairs which ones they visit most often and why. Most pairs spoke enthusiastically, mixing Arabic and English. Ms D. then elicited some vocabulary from the whole class and wrote key words on the board, including "menu," "order," "bill," and "waiter."

**0:10-0:30 Vocabulary presentation**
Ms D. distributed a handout with 20 food and restaurant vocabulary items, each with a picture and definition. She read through each item, drilling pronunciation with the class. She asked students to categorise the words into groups: ordering food, describing food, and restaurant equipment. Students worked in pairs on this. Ms D. monitored but tended to stay near the front rows, and students at the back appeared to be copying from their neighbours rather than discussing.

**0:30-0:45 Controlled practice**
Students completed a matching exercise pairing vocabulary to definitions, then a gap-fill exercise using the target vocabulary in short dialogues. Ms D. checked answers with the whole class. When students gave incorrect answers, she provided the correct answer immediately without exploring why the error occurred.

**0:45-0:55 Break**

**0:55-1:10 Listening activity**
Ms D. played an audio recording of two people ordering food at a restaurant. Students listened for specific vocabulary from the lesson. After listening twice, students checked in pairs. Ms D. went through the answers. She played the audio a third time for one question where most students had the wrong answer.

**1:10-1:25 Role play**
Ms D. paired students and assigned roles: customer and waiter. She gave a brief demonstration with one student. Pairs practised a restaurant dialogue using a prompt card with key phrases. Ms D. circulated and listened but offered minimal feedback during the activity. Several pairs finished quickly and sat idle, while two pairs struggled with the task. Ms D. did not regroup or provide an extension task.

**1:25-1:30 Wrap-up**
Ms D. asked three pairs to perform their role play for the class. She praised each performance with "very good." No specific language feedback was given. The lesson ended with Ms D. assigning vocabulary review for homework.

---

**General notes:** Materials were well-prepared and visually engaging. Students seemed to enjoy the topic. The classroom was equipped with a projector and speakers.
