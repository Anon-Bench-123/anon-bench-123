# Lesson Observation Notes

**Teacher:** Mr E.
**Date:** 4 February 2026
**Class:** 24 primary learners, aged 9-10, A1 level
**Lesson:** English - Animals Vocabulary (40 minutes)
**Observer:** Head of Primary English Department

---

## Observation

**0:00-0:05 Opening**
Mr E. greeted the class and asked them to sit in their seats. He held up a picture of a lion and asked "What animal is this?" Several students called out "lion" excitedly. He then held up pictures of five more animals (elephant, giraffe, zebra, monkey, snake) one by one, eliciting the English words. Students who knew the words shouted them out, while quieter students did not participate.

**0:05-0:15 Presentation**
Mr E. stuck the six animal pictures on the board and wrote the English word under each. He drilled the pronunciation of each word with the whole class. He then asked students to repeat the words while pointing to each picture. The drilling was enthusiastic and students seemed engaged. However, Mr E. did not check individual pronunciation or address common errors (several students were saying "giraff" without the final sound).

**0:15-0:25 Activity 1 - Matching**
Mr E. distributed a worksheet with pictures of the six animals and their names jumbled. Students drew lines to match them. Most students completed this in about three minutes. Mr E. told the class to wait quietly until everyone finished. Faster students became restless, some poking each other. After five minutes of waiting, Mr E. checked the answers by calling on students to read their answers aloud.

**0:25-0:35 Activity 2 - Drawing**
Mr E. asked students to draw their favourite animal and write the English word underneath. Students enjoyed this activity. Mr E. walked around looking at drawings and saying "very nice" to each student. He did not use this opportunity to extend language practice beyond the single-word level.

**0:35-0:40 Closing**
Mr E. asked five students to hold up their drawings and say the animal name. He then told the class to remember the six words for next lesson. There was no review game or other consolidation activity.

---

**General notes:** Mr E. had good energy and was warm with the students. The classroom had no projector; only the whiteboard and printed materials were available. Students had no individual devices.
