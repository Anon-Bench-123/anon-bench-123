Trainer notes -- Learner-14 (corporate English programme, Tokyo office)

Placement (April): Placed at strong B1. Reading and grammar solid for level. Writing showed limited range of linking words and frequent errors with articles. Listening adequate but learner reported difficulty following native speakers in meetings.

Module 1 end (May): Grammar improving -- more confident with modals. Reading remains a strength. Writing still shows the same issues from placement: article errors, limited connectors, and tendency to write very short sentences. Learner mentioned that writing tasks feel stressful because of time pressure. Listening score improved slightly.

Mid-course (July): Good progress in grammar and reading. Reading score now approaching B2 standard. Writing score barely changed -- learner produced a report that was understandable but lacked cohesion and had repeated errors with definite/indefinite articles and subject-verb agreement in longer sentences. Learner expressed frustration about writing not improving despite practice. Listening steady.

Module 3 end (September): Grammar and reading continue to develop well. Listening dipped on the teleconference task -- learner said the mixed accents were very difficult. Writing improved slightly on the complaint email -- structure was better but still had article and agreement errors. Learner has started reading English news articles outside class, which seems to be helping vocabulary and reading confidence.

End-of-course (November): Strong finish in grammar and reading -- both approaching or at B2 level. Listening recovered and improved. Writing showed modest improvement overall but remains the weakest area. The business proposal was well-organised but still contained persistent article errors and some awkward phrasing. Learner has made clear progress overall but writing development has been much slower than other skills.
