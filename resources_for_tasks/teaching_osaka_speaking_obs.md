# Lesson Observation Notes

**Teacher**: Ms T. (CELTA, 3 years teaching experience)
**Class**: A2 Adult General English, 9 students
**Institute**: Corporate language training centre, Osaka, Japan
**Date**: [redacted]
**Lesson duration**: 45 minutes
**Topic**: Speaking -- describing past experiences (past simple)
**Observer**: Senior Teacher

---

## Stage 1: Warm-up (0:00-0:05)

Teacher showed a projected photo of herself on holiday and said three sentences about it: "I visited Kyoto last month. I ate amazing food. I saw beautiful temples." She asked students "What did I do?" and elicited the three activities. Good energy. Students were attentive.

## Stage 2: Language review (0:05-0:15)

Teacher projected a table of 12 irregular past simple forms (go-went, eat-ate, see-saw, buy-bought, take-took, have-had, make-made, come-came, give-gave, drink-drank, read-read, write-wrote). Students repeated each form chorally. Teacher then played a quick drilling game: she said the base form, students called out the past form. Pace was good and students seemed to enjoy the challenge.

Teacher then asked individual students to make one sentence each using a past form. About half the class contributed. The other students listened but were not called on.

## Stage 3: Controlled practice -- gap-fill (0:15-0:22)

Teacher distributed a worksheet with 8 sentences, each with a gap requiring the correct past simple form. Students worked individually. Teacher circulated briefly, then returned to the front to set up the next activity on the projector. After about 5 minutes, she asked students to compare answers with a partner, then went through answers as a class. Answers were mostly correct.

## Stage 4: Freer practice -- pair interviews (0:22-0:38)

Teacher told students to ask their partner about their last holiday or weekend. She said "Ask questions like: Where did you go? What did you do? Did you enjoy it?" Students worked in pairs. The room was quiet. Most pairs spoke in short, minimal exchanges (2-3 word answers). Several long pauses were observed.

Teacher circulated and listened but did not intervene when pairs fell silent. She did not model the activity first or provide a demonstration exchange with a student. No prompt cards, question lists, or visual aids were available to support students who ran out of things to say.

One pair finished after about 6 minutes and sat in silence. Teacher asked them to switch partners but by then most pairs were also finishing. The activity felt like it ran out of momentum halfway through.

When students made errors during the pair work, teacher occasionally corrected immediately by saying the correct form. For example, one student said "I goed to Nara" and the teacher said "went -- I went to Nara" before moving to the next pair. No delayed error correction or error noting was observed.

## Stage 5: Round-up (0:38-0:45)

Teacher asked three students to tell the class one thing their partner did. Responses were brief ("She went to Osaka"). Teacher praised each response. She then said "Good job today, past simple is very important." No error correction stage, no feedback on the speaking activity, no review of the language from the lesson.

---

## General notes

- Teacher is friendly and well-prepared with clear materials
- The warm-up was personalised and effective
- The drilling game was engaging and well-paced
- The controlled practice went smoothly
- The freer practice stage lacked sufficient scaffolding for A2 learners to sustain a conversation
- Error correction during speaking was exclusively on-the-spot recasting with no delayed correction or error analysis
- Students who were not called on during the language review stage had no opportunity to produce language until the pair work
- The transition from controlled practice to freer practice was abrupt, with no guided or semi-controlled stage bridging the gap
- Teacher seemed unsure how to support pairs when conversation stalled
