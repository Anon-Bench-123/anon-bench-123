# Lesson Observation Notes

**Teacher**: Ms T. (2 years teaching experience, Bachelor of Secondary Education major in English)
**Class**: A1 Primary, 38 students aged 9-10
**School**: Public elementary school, rural Leyte province, Philippines
**Date**: [redacted]
**Lesson duration**: 40 minutes
**Topic**: Vocabulary -- animals and their habitats
**Observer**: School Principal

---

## Stage 1: Review and introduction (0:00-0:10)

Teacher asked the class to stand up and sing the "Head, Shoulders, Knees and Toes" song together. Students were enthusiastic and energetic. Teacher then said "Today we will learn about animals." She held up a hand-drawn poster showing six animals: dog, cat, fish, bird, cow, frog. She pointed to each animal, said the English word, and asked the class to repeat three times. Students repeated loudly in chorus.

Teacher then held up a second poster with three habitats drawn: a pond, a farm, and a forest. She pointed to each and said the English word. Students repeated.

## Stage 2: Matching activity (0:10-0:20)

Teacher asked students to copy the six animals and three habitats into their notebooks and draw lines connecting each animal to its habitat. Students worked individually. The classroom had no projector and the posters were small, so students sitting at the back of the room (rows 5-7) had difficulty seeing the images. Several students stood up and walked to the front to look more closely, causing some disruption.

Teacher circulated but the class was large and she only reached about half the students during this stage. She helped individual students by pointing to the correct habitat on the poster. Some students finished quickly and began chatting in Waray-Waray with their neighbours while others were still working.

## Stage 3: Whole-class check (0:20-0:28)

Teacher asked volunteers to come to the board and write which habitat each animal lives in. Six students volunteered. Teacher confirmed each answer and the class applauded each student. She corrected one student who placed "fish" with "farm" by saying "No, fish lives in water" and pointing to the pond.

No further explanation was given about why animals live in particular habitats. The word "habitat" itself was not explicitly taught or checked for understanding.

## Stage 4: Sentence writing (0:28-0:38)

Teacher wrote a model sentence on the board: "A dog lives on a farm." She asked students to write similar sentences for all six animals. Many students struggled with the sentence structure. Some wrote only the animal name and the habitat without a sentence frame. Teacher circulated and helped students individually but time was limited.

After 8 minutes, teacher asked three students to read their sentences aloud. Two read correctly. One student read "A bird live in forest" and teacher said "Good try" but did not correct the missing article or the verb agreement.

## Stage 5: Close (0:38-0:40)

Teacher told students they did a good job and asked them to finish any incomplete sentences for homework. She reminded them to bring their notebooks next class.

---

## General notes

- Teacher is clearly enthusiastic and has a warm relationship with the students
- Hand-drawn posters showed creativity given the lack of resources
- The large class size made individual attention during writing very difficult
- Early finishers had no extension task and became restless
- The sentence-writing stage was ambitious for A1 learners who had only just encountered the vocabulary
- Error correction was inconsistent: some errors were addressed, others were praised without correction
- No scaffolding was provided between vocabulary introduction and sentence production
- The concept of "habitat" was used as an organising principle but was not itself taught
