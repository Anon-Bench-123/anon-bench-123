# Lesson Observation Notes

**Teacher**: Mr N. (8 years teaching experience, Licence in English Studies, University of Dakar)
**Class**: B1 Upper-Secondary, 22 students aged 16-17
**School**: State lycee, Thies, Senegal
**Date**: [redacted]
**Lesson duration**: 45 minutes
**Topic**: Reading comprehension -- a newspaper article about climate change
**Observer**: English Department Inspector

---

## Stage 1: Pre-reading (0:00-0:10)

Teacher wrote "CLIMATE CHANGE" on the board and asked students what they knew about the topic. Four students volunteered answers in English, though with some use of French words mixed in. Teacher accepted the French words and translated them into English, writing key vocabulary on the board: "pollution", "temperature", "greenhouse", "flood", "drought". Teacher asked students to copy these words into their notebooks.

Teacher did not ask students to predict the content of the article, nor did he activate prior knowledge through discussion questions or visual prompts.

## Stage 2: Reading the article (0:10-0:25)

Teacher distributed photocopies of a newspaper article (approximately 350 words, taken from an English-language newspaper). He asked students to read the article silently and underline any words they did not understand. Students read for about 5 minutes.

Teacher then asked the class to identify unknown words. Students called out about 12 words. Teacher translated each word directly into French and wrote the French equivalent next to the English word on the board. He did not attempt to explain any words in English first, nor did he encourage students to guess meaning from context.

After the vocabulary clarification, teacher read the entire article aloud to the class while students followed along. He read at a natural pace without pausing to check comprehension during the reading.

## Stage 3: Comprehension questions (0:25-0:38)

Teacher wrote five comprehension questions on the board:
1. What is the main topic of the article?
2. What are two causes of climate change mentioned?
3. What effects of climate change does the article describe?
4. What solutions does the article suggest?
5. Do you agree with the article? Why or why not?

Students answered in their notebooks individually. Teacher circulated and looked at student work. He stopped at one student's desk and corrected a grammar error in their written response, but this interaction was in French.

After about 10 minutes, teacher asked for volunteers to share answers. Three students answered questions 1-4. For question 5 (the opinion question), teacher asked two students to share. Both gave very brief responses of one sentence. Teacher did not follow up with further discussion or ask other students to respond to their classmates' opinions.

## Stage 4: Close (0:38-0:45)

Teacher told students to finish any remaining answers for homework and to study the new vocabulary for a quiz next week. He reviewed the five vocabulary words from the board quickly and ended the lesson.

---

## General notes

- Teacher is confident in his own English and has good subject knowledge
- Classroom was calm and students were generally on-task
- The lesson was heavily teacher-centred with limited student-to-student interaction
- The approach to unknown vocabulary was exclusively translation-based with no strategy training
- Comprehension questions were appropriate in range (factual to inferential to evaluative) but the evaluative question was under-exploited
- No pair or group work was used at any point during the lesson
- The reading was treated as a vehicle for vocabulary rather than for developing reading skills or strategies
- Teacher's use of French for classroom management and vocabulary explanation was frequent; English was used mainly for reading aloud and giving instructions
