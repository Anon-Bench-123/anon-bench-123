# Lesson Observation Notes

**Teacher**: Mr D. (5 years teaching experience, CELTA qualified)
**Class**: A2 Lower-Secondary, 26 students aged 13-14
**School**: State secondary school, Valencia, Spain
**Date**: [redacted]
**Lesson duration**: 55 minutes
**Topic**: Past simple -- regular and irregular verbs
**Observer**: Head of English Department

---

## Stage 1: Warm-up (0:00-0:07)

Teacher greeted students in English. He asked "What did you do at the weekend?" and nominated three students to answer. Two gave short answers using Spanish when they could not think of the English verb. Teacher translated for them and wrote the English verb on the board. Third student gave a longer answer in English. Teacher praised all three. Most of the class was quiet and watching.

## Stage 2: Grammar presentation (0:07-0:25)

Teacher opened the textbook to the grammar reference page and asked students to do the same. He read aloud the rules for forming the past simple with regular verbs (-ed ending) and then listed 15 irregular verbs on the board with their past forms. He asked the class to repeat each verb after him twice. He then explained the rules for negative and question forms using "did/didn't" and wrote example sentences on the board.

Throughout this stage, teacher spoke almost entirely in English at a pace that appeared fast for several students. Two students in the middle rows had their heads down and were not following. Teacher did not check whether students could distinguish between regular and irregular verb formation. No visual timeline or other aid was used to illustrate the concept of past time.

## Stage 3: Textbook exercise (0:25-0:40)

Teacher asked students to complete exercise 3 on page 42: a gap-fill activity with 10 sentences requiring the correct past simple form. Students worked individually in silence. Teacher sat at his desk and checked his computer for approximately 5 minutes before beginning to circulate. When he circulated, he checked answers by looking over shoulders and saying "good" or "no, check again" but did not explain why an answer was incorrect.

After 12 minutes, teacher stopped the activity and read out the correct answers. Students self-corrected. No discussion of common errors or patterns.

## Stage 4: Speaking activity (0:40-0:50)

Teacher put students in pairs and gave this instruction: "Now talk to your partner about what you did yesterday. Use the past simple." He repeated the instruction once in English but did not model an example exchange. Some pairs began talking immediately but several pairs appeared uncertain about what to say. Five pairs switched to Spanish within the first minute. Teacher circulated and reminded students to speak English but did not provide any language support or prompts.

After about 7 minutes, teacher asked two pairs to share what their partner had said. Both pairs gave very short responses of one or two sentences.

## Stage 5: Homework and close (0:50-0:55)

Teacher assigned textbook exercises 4 and 5 on page 43 for homework. He said "Any questions? No? OK, see you on Wednesday" and the lesson ended.

---

## General notes

- Teacher has a friendly manner and students seem to like him
- Classroom was orderly throughout with no behavioural issues
- The transition from controlled practice straight to free speaking with no intermediate guided stage was abrupt
- Teacher talking time was very high during the presentation stage; student talking time was low overall
- L1 use by students during the speaking activity was not effectively managed
- No checking of learning was conducted at any point beyond the gap-fill self-correction
- The pace of teacher speech during presentation may have been too fast for several A2 learners in the group
