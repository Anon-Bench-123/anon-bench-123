# Writing Sample: Argumentative Essay Excerpt

**Student**: student-1, age 17, Upper-Secondary
**Task**: Write an argumentative essay (250-300 words) on the topic: "Should schools replace traditional exams with project-based assessments?"

---

## Student's Essay

The debate over whether schools should move away from traditional exams in favour of project-based assessments has intensified in recent years. While both methods have their merits, I would argue that a gradual shift towards project-based assessment is both desirable and necessary, provided that certain safeguards remain in place.

The primary limitation of traditional exams is that they tend to reward memorisation over genuine understanding. A student who can reproduce information under timed conditions may not necessarily be able to apply that knowledge in a meaningful way. In contrast, project-based assessments require students to engage with material over an extended period, synthesising information from multiple sources and demonstrating practical competence. For instance, a science project that involves designing an experiment, collecting data, and presenting conclusions mirrors the kind of reasoning that employers and universities increasingly value.

However, it would be unwise to abandon exams entirely. Standardised testing provides a level of comparability that project work cannot easily replicate. When universities or employers need to compare candidates from different schools or regions, a common examination framework offers a transparent benchmark. Furthermore, there is a risk that project assessments may inadvertently disadvantage students who lack access to resources at home, such as reliable internet or parental support.

A balanced approach would therefore seem the most sensible. Schools could allocate perhaps sixty percent of assessment weighting to project-based work, while retaining exams for core areas where standardised measurement is important. This would allow students to develop deeper analytical skills without sacrificing the accountability that examinations provide.

In conclusion, the question is not whether project-based assessment should replace exams, but rather how the two can coexist. A thoughtful combination of both methods would better serve the diverse needs of learners and prepare them more effectively for life beyond school.
