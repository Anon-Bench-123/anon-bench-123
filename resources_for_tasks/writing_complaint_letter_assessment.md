# Writing Assessment: Formal Complaint Letter

**Student**: B2 adult learner, aged 30-40, Islamabad, Pakistan
**Task**: Formal complaint letter to airline
**Key Issues**: Formal register maintenance, indirect polite requests, passive voice usage

---

## Student's Letter (450 words)

Fahad Ahmed
123 Garden Town
Islamabad, Pakistan
Email: fahmed@email.com

October 20, 2025

Customer Service Department
Global Airways
Airport Road
Islamabad

Dear Sir or Madam,

I am writing to complain about the very bad service I got on my recent flight from Islamabad to Dubai on October 15, 2025 (Flight GA402). I am very disappointed and angry about how your airline treated me and other passengers.

First of all, the flight was delayed for 4 hours without any proper explanation. When I arrived at the airport at 2:00 PM for my 4:00 PM flight, the departure board showed "Delayed" but nobody from your staff told us why or for how long. I asked several staff members at the check-in desk and the gate, but they didn't know anything or just ignored me. This was really frustrating because I had an important business meeting in Dubai that evening which I missed because of your delay.

When we finally boarded the plane at 8:00 PM, there were more problems. My seat 14A had a broken entertainment system, and the tray table was dirty with food stains from the previous flight. I called the flight attendant and showed her these problems, but she just said "Sorry, we can't fix it now" and walked away. This was very rude and unprofessional.

The food service was also terrible. The meal they served was cold and tasted bad. When I complained about it, the flight attendant got annoyed and said that if I don't like it, I don't have to eat it. This kind of attitude is unacceptable from airline staff.

To make matters worse, when we landed in Dubai, they lost one of my checked bags. I waited at the baggage carousel for over an hour, but my bag never came. When I went to the lost baggage counter, the staff were unhelpful and made me fill out many forms. They said they will try to find my bag and deliver it to my hotel, but it took 3 days to arrive! My business clothes and important documents were in that bag, which caused me serious problems during my trip.

I am a frequent flyer and I have never experienced such poor service from any airline before. This experience has made me very upset and I don't want to fly with Global Airways again unless you do something about it.

I want you to give me a full refund for my ticket and compensation for the inconvenience and the business opportunities I lost because of your bad service. I also think you should improve your staff training and customer service procedures.

I want your response within 14 days. If I don't hear from you, I will have to complain to the Civil Aviation Authority.

Yours faithfully,
Fahad Ahmed

---

**Strengths**: Appropriate letter format, clear chronological description of problems, mentions specific flight details
**Areas for Improvement**:
- Formal register: Frequent lapses into informal language - "very bad service" (should be "unsatisfactory/inadequate service"), "got" (should be "received"), "really frustrating" (should be "extremely frustrating" or "highly frustrating"), "terrible" (too informal - use "unacceptable")
- Direct negative language: "I am very disappointed and angry" (too emotional/direct for formal complaint - should focus on facts), "This was very rude" (should be "This response was inappropriate"), "tasted bad" (use "was of poor quality")
- Indirect polite requests: "I want you to give me a full refund" (too direct - should be "I am requesting a full refund"), "I want your response" (should be "I would appreciate your response" or "I expect to receive your response")
- Passive voice: Overuses active voice where passive would be more diplomatic - "they lost one of my checked bags" (better: "one of my checked bags was lost"), "the staff were unhelpful" (better: "assistance was not provided")
- Threats: "I will have to complain to the Civil Aviation Authority" is too aggressive - should be softer: "I may be compelled to escalate this matter"
