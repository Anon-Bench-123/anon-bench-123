# Writing Assessment: Environmental Issues Essay

**Student**: B2 university student, aged 18-21, San Jose, Costa Rica
**Task**: Argumentative essay about environmental issues
**Key Issues**: Academic vocabulary variety, conditional sentences, integrating supporting evidence

## Student's Essay (550 words)

The Urgent Need for Individual Action on Climate Change

Climate change is one of the biggest challenges of our time. Many people think that only governments and big companies can solve this problem, but I believe that individual actions are also very important. In this essay, I will argue that personal choices make a real difference in fighting climate change.

First, individuals have significant impact through their daily consumption habits. Every time we buy something, we are supporting a particular industry. If we buy products that are environmentally friendly, we encourage companies to produce more sustainable goods. For example, when we choose to buy local food instead of imported food, we reduce carbon emissions from transportation. Statistics show that food transportation accounts for approximately 11% of food-related greenhouse gas emissions. If many people make this choice, it sends a strong message to the market.

Second, individual transportation choices matter a lot. Cars are responsible for large amount of pollution in cities. If people use public transportation, bikes, or walk instead of driving, the impact would be huge. Research from European Environment Agency shows that if people reduce their car use by just 20%, it could reduce urban air pollution by up to 15%. This would make big difference for air quality and public health. However, I understand that not everyone can stop using their car completely, especially in areas where public transport is not good. But even small changes help.

Some people argue that individual actions are too small to matter. They say that big corporations produce most of the pollution, so individuals should not feel responsible. While it is true that major companies produce large emissions, this argument ignores the fact that these companies exist to serve consumers. If consumers change their habits, companies will change too. When people stopped buying products with CFCs in the 1980s, companies stopped producing them. This shows that consumer choices do influence corporate behavior.

Another important area where individuals can act is energy consumption at home. Many households waste energy by leaving lights on, using old appliances, or heating or cooling their homes too much. If families invest in energy-efficient appliances and be more careful about their energy use, they can reduce their carbon footprint by significant amounts. Studies show that energy-efficient homes use 50% less energy than traditional homes. If everyone in developed countries would reduce their home energy use by 30%, it would be equivalent to removing millions of cars from roads.

Education and awareness are also key. When individuals learn about environmental issues and share this knowledge with others, they multiply the impact. Social media makes it easy to spread information quickly. One person's action can inspire many others. If a family starts recycling or composting, their neighbors might do the same.

Of course, individual action alone is not enough. We definitely need government policies and corporate responsibility too. But individuals should not wait for governments to solve everything. We can start making changes now.

In conclusion, individual actions do make difference in fighting climate change. Through our consumption choices, transportation habits, energy use, and by spreading awareness, each person can contribute to solving this global problem. The key is that many individuals need to act together. Climate change is everyone's problem, and everyone must be part of the solution.

**Strengths**: Clear thesis, good logical structure, attempts to use evidence
**Areas for Improvement**:
- Academic vocabulary: Repetitive use of basic words ("big," "a lot," "very") - could use "significant," "substantial," "considerable"
- Conditionals: Some issues with conditional forms - "If families invest...and be more careful" (should be "and are/were"), "If everyone...would reduce" (mixing real and unreal conditionals inconsistently)
- Evidence integration: Statistics mentioned but not smoothly integrated - "Statistics show that..." is repetitive, could vary with "According to...", "Research indicates..."
