# Writing Sample: Academic Essay

**Student**: student-1, Age 22, University English course
**Task**: Write an academic essay (250-300 words) discussing whether globalisation has had a positive or negative impact on local cultures. Support your argument with examples.

---

## Student's Writing

Globalisation has transformed the way societies interact, and its effects on local cultures remain a subject of considerable debate. While some argue that globalisation enriches cultural exchange, I would contend that its impact on local traditions has been predominantly negative, though not without certain benefits.

The most significant concern is the homogenisation of cultural practices. As multinational corporations expand their reach, local businesses and traditions are frequently displaced. For instance, in many Asian cities, traditional family-run restaurants have been replaced by international fast-food chains, which not only alter dietary habits but also diminish the social rituals that was associated with communal dining. This pattern of cultural erosion is replicated across numerous domains, from entertainment to fashion.

Furthermore, the dominance of English as a global lingua franca poses a threat to linguistic diversity. According to UNESCO, approximately one language disappears every two weeks, and while globalisation is not the sole cause, it accelerates the process by privileging economically dominant languages. Young people in many countries increasingly perceive their native language as less prestigious than English, which leading to a gradual abandonment of linguistic heritage.

However, it would be an oversimplification to dismiss globalisation entirely. Digital connectivity has enabled marginalised communities to share their cultural practices with a global audience. Indigenous artists, for example, has gained international recognition through social media platforms, thereby preserving traditions that might otherwise have been forgotten.

In conclusion, while globalisation offers certain opportunities for cultural visibility, the evidence suggests that its overall impact on local cultures is more harmful than beneficial. The challenge for policymakers is to harness the advantages of global interconnectedness whilst implementing measures that safeguard cultural diversity. Without such interventions, the rich tapestry of human culture risks being reduced to a uniform global monoculture.
