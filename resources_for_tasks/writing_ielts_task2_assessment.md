# IELTS Writing Task 2 Sample Essays - B2 Students (Dhaka, Bangladesh)

## Context
**Course:** IELTS Preparation - Writing Module
**Level:** B2 (Current), Target 6.5-7.0 band
**Students:** 18 students from Bangladesh preparing for IELTS
**Time Remaining:** 6 weeks

---

## IELTS Writing Task 2 Prompt

**Write about the following topic:**

> Some people believe that university students should study whatever they like. Others believe that they should only be allowed to study subjects that will be useful in the future, such as those related to science and technology.
>
> Discuss both these views and give your own opinion.

**Write at least 250 words.**

---

## Sample Essay 1: Band 5.5-6.0

In the modern world, education is very important for everyone. Some people think university students should study what they like, but others think they should study useful subjects like science and technology. This essay will discuss both views and give my opinion.

Firstly, some people believe students should study whatever subject they like. This is because when students study what they interest in, they will be more motivated and work harder. Also, every subject has its own value. Art, music, and literature are also important for society, not only science and technology.

On the other hand, many people think students should only study useful subjects like science and technology. In Bangladesh and many developing countries, there are many engineering and IT jobs available. If students study computer science or engineering, they can easily get high-paying jobs and support their families.

In my opinion, I think students should be free to choose their subjects, but they should also think about future jobs. Universities should offer all subjects, but students should understand which subjects have good job prospects.

In conclusion, both views have merit. While students should have freedom to choose their subjects, they should also consider practical aspects of their future careers.


---

## Sample Essay 2: Band 6.5-7.0

The question of whether university students should study subjects of their choice or focus on practical fields like science and technology is widely debated. While some people advocate for academic freedom, others emphasize the importance of employability. This essay will discuss both perspectives before presenting my own view.

On one hand, allowing students to pursue subjects they are passionate about has several advantages. When students study what genuinely interests them, they are more likely to excel academically and develop deep expertise in their field. Furthermore, a diverse range of academic disciplines is essential for a well-rounded society. Arts, humanities, and social sciences provide critical perspectives on human behavior, history, and culture that are just as valuable as scientific knowledge.

On the other hand, there are compelling arguments for prioritizing practical subjects. In today's competitive job market, graduates with degrees in STEM fields often have better employment prospects and higher earning potential. In Bangladesh, for example, the technology sector is rapidly growing, and there is high demand for software developers and engineers. From an economic perspective, encouraging students to study these subjects can help address skills shortages and drive national development.

In my opinion, while practical considerations are important, students should not be forced to study subjects that do not align with their abilities and interests. A balanced approach would be to provide comprehensive career guidance and interdisciplinary programs that combine traditional subjects with marketable skills.

To conclude, although subjects like science and technology offer clear practical benefits, restricting students' academic freedom could be counterproductive. The ideal solution is to empower students with information and skills while respecting their individual aspirations.
