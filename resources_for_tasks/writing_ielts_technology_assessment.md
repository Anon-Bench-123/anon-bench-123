# Writing Assessment: IELTS Task 2 Essay

**Student**: B2 adult learner, aged 22-28, Kathmandu, Nepal
**Task**: IELTS Task 2 practice essay on technology
**Key Issues**: Task response completeness, sentence variety, article usage

---

## IELTS Task 2 Prompt

Some people believe that technology has made our lives more complicated, while others think it has made life easier. Discuss both views and give your own opinion.

Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your own knowledge or experience.

Write at least 250 words.

---

## Student's Essay

Technology and Modern Life

In today's world, technology is everywhere and affects every aspect of our lives. Some people argue that technology creates more problems than it solves, while others believe that it improves our quality of life. This essay will examine both perspectives before presenting my own view.

On one hand, technology has made many tasks much easier and faster. For instance, communication has become incredibly simple. People can now contact anyone in the world instantly through smartphones and internet. This saves time and money compared to traditional methods like letters or expensive international phone calls. Moreover, technology has improved healthcare significantly. Doctors can diagnose diseases more accurately using advanced equipment, and many medical procedures that were once dangerous are now routine. Online shopping is another convenience that technology has brought, allowing people to buy products from home instead of spending hours in crowded stores.

However, there are valid concerns about negative impacts of technology. Many people feel overwhelmed by constant connectivity. Smartphones and computers demand our attention all day long with notifications, emails, and messages. This makes it difficult to relax or focus on single task. Furthermore, technology has created new problems such as cybercrime, identity theft, and online fraud that didn't exist before. People must now worry about protecting their personal information and financial details online. Another issue is that technology can make people lazy and less social. Instead of exercising or meeting friends, many individuals spend hours watching videos or playing games alone.

Additionally, the rapid pace of technological change means that people must constantly learn new skills and adapt to new systems. This is stressful, especially for older people who did not grow up with computers. The workplace has become more demanding because employees are expected to be available and productive at all times through mobile devices and email.

In my opinion, while technology does create some complications, overall it has made life considerably easier. The key is to use technology wisely and maintain balance. We should take advantage of the conveniences it offers while also setting boundaries to protect our mental health and relationships. For example, I personally benefit from online learning platforms that allow me to study English from Nepal without traveling abroad, but I also make sure to turn off my phone during meals and before bedtime.

In conclusion, technology is tool that can either simplify or complicate our lives depending on how we use it. By being mindful of both the benefits and drawbacks, we can harness technology to improve our lives while avoiding its negative effects.

**Word Count**: 421 words

**Strengths**: Addresses both sides of the argument, provides clear opinion, good range of examples, appropriate length for IELTS
**Areas for Improvement**:
- Task response: Could develop the "technology makes life complicated" side more thoroughly - spends more words on the "easier" side
- Article usage: "through smartphones and internet" (missing "the"), "on single task" (missing "a"), "technology is tool" (missing "a")
- Sentence variety: Overreliance on simple sentences in second paragraph - "This saves time and money. Moreover, technology has improved. Doctors can diagnose. This makes it difficult." Could combine with complex structures
- Coherence: Paragraph 3 could be split - first part about connectivity/stress, second part about crime, third part about social issues - too many ideas in one paragraph
- Minor: "incredibly simple" is too informal for IELTS academic writing
