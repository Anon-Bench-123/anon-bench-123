# Writing Sample: IELTS Task 2 Essay

**Student**: student-1, Age 28, Adult IELTS Preparation course
**Task**: Some people believe that governments should invest more in public transport rather than building new roads for private vehicles. To what extent do you agree or disagree? Write at least 250 words.

---

## Student's Writing

In recent years, the question of how governments should allocate their budget for transportation has become increasingly relevant. While some argue that building new roads for private vehicles is necessary, I strongly agree that investing in public transport is a better approach for several reasons.

Firstly, public transport is more environmental friendly than private cars. Buses and trains can carry hundreds of passengers at the same time, which reduces the number of individual vehicles on the road. For example, in cities like Curitiba in Brazil, the investment in an efficient bus system has significantly reduced carbon emissions. If governments continue to build more roads, this will only encourage more people to drive, which makes the pollution problem even more worse.

Secondly, public transport provides equal access for all citizens, including those who cannot afford to own a car. In many developing countries, a large percentage of the population relies on buses and trains to get to work and school. Building more roads primarily benefits the wealthier segments of the society who own vehicles. Therefore, investing in public transport is a more fair way to spend public money.

On the other hand, it is important to recognise that some regions, particularly rural areas, may still need new roads because public transport is not always feasible in places with low population density. However, this does not mean that the overall priority should be road construction.

In conclusion, I believe that governments should prioritise public transport investment because it is better for the environment and promotes social equality. Although road building may be necessary in certain situations, it should not receive more funding as public transport systems that benefit the majority of citizens.
