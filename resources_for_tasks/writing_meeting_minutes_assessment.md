# Writing Assessment: Meeting Minutes

**Student**: B2 professional, aged 26-35, Male, Maldives
**Task**: Recording minutes from team meeting
**Key Issues**: Passive voice for formal documentation, reported speech, action item clarity

## Student's Meeting Minutes (500 words)

MEETING MINUTES

**Project**: Website Redesign Project
**Date**: October 18, 2025
**Time**: 10:00 AM - 11:30 AM
**Location**: Conference Room B / Virtual (Zoom)
**Chair**: Sarah Johnson, Project Manager
**Attendees**: Ahmed Hassan (Lead Developer), Maria Santos (UX Designer), John Kim (Content Manager), Lisa Patel (Marketing Director)
**Absent**: Tom Wilson (excused - business travel)

---

**1. Opening and Approval of Previous Minutes**

Sarah Johnson started the meeting at 10:05 AM. She welcomed everyone and asked if everyone read the minutes from last meeting. Everyone said they had read them. The minutes were approved without any changes.

**2. Progress Updates**

**a) Design Phase**
Maria presented the new design mockups for the homepage and product pages. She explained that she has finished all the main pages except the checkout page, which will be ready by next Friday. The team discussed the color scheme, and several people said that the blue was too dark. Maria said she will make the blue lighter and will share updated designs by Wednesday.

**b) Development Status**
Ahmed reported about the backend development. He said that the database migration is completed and the new server is set up. However, he mentioned that he is having problems with the payment gateway integration. He said that the payment gateway provider has been slow to respond to his technical questions. Sarah suggested that she will contact the provider's account manager to speed up the support. Ahmed agreed this would be helpful.

**c) Content Migration**
John gave an update on content migration. He told that his team has migrated about 60% of the existing content to the new system. He explained that some product descriptions need to be rewritten to match the new style guide. He asked if he can hire a freelance writer to help with this. Sarah said that she will check the budget and let him know by tomorrow.

**3. Discussion Items**

**a) Launch Timeline**
The team discussed the launch date. Originally, they planned to launch on November 15, but given the delays with payment gateway integration, this date seems unrealistic now. After discussion, they decided to postpone the launch to November 30. This will give Ahmed extra time to solve the payment issues and allow John's team to finish content migration properly.

Lisa expressed concern about the delay impacting the holiday season marketing campaign. She said that the campaign is planned to start on December 1. After discussion, everyone agreed that launching on November 30 is still acceptable for the campaign.

**b) Budget Review**
Sarah presented the current budget status. She reported that the project spent 65% of the allocated budget so far, which is higher than expected at this stage. The main reason is that they had to hire additional developers to help Ahmed. Lisa suggested that they should cut some planned features to stay within budget. After lengthy discussion, they agreed to postpone the implementation of the wishlist feature to Phase 2 after the initial launch.

**4. Action Items**

The following action items were assigned:

2. Sarah - Contact payment gateway account manager (Due: October 19)
3. Sarah - Check budget for freelance writer and inform John (Due: October 19)
4. Ahmed - Continue working on payment gateway with support from provider (Due: October 30)
5. John - Complete content migration (Due: November 20)
6. All team members - Review revised timeline document (Due: October 25)

**5. Next Meeting**

The next meeting is scheduled for October 25, 2025 at 10:00 AM in Conference Room B.

**6. Adjournment**

Sarah adjourned the meeting at 11:35 AM.

**Strengths**: Professional format with all necessary elements, clear structure, comprehensive coverage of discussion
**Areas for Improvement**:
- Passive voice: Should use more passive constructions for formal documentation - "Sarah started the meeting" → "The meeting was opened by Sarah Johnson," "Everyone said they had read them" → "The previous minutes were reviewed and approved," "Maria presented" → "The design mockups were presented"
- Reported speech: Inconsistent use - "He said that the database migration is completed" (mixing tenses - should be "was completed"), "She said that she will contact" (should be "would contact")
- Action item clarity: Good list but could be more specific - "Continue working on payment gateway" is vague (what specific tasks/milestones?)
- Wordiness: "After discussion" appears 3 times - vary with "Following deliberation," "The team agreed," "After consideration"
- Minor: "Sarah suggested that she will contact" (formal minutes typically avoid "suggest" in this context - use "Sarah agreed to contact"), "Ahmed agreed this would be helpful" (unnecessary detail for minutes)
