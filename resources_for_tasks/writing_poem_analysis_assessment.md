## Student's Essay (Excerpt - 450 words)

Analysis of "The Road Not Taken" by Robert Frost

The poem "The Road Not Taken" by Robert Frost is one of most famous poems in American literature. It was published in 1916 and it explores theme of choices and their consequences in human life. In this essay, I will analyze the poem's meaning, imagery, and its message about decision-making.

The poem describes speaker who stands at fork in wood where two roads diverge. He must choose which road to take. This situation is metaphor for the choices that people face in their lives. The speaker looks down one road as far as he can see, but then he chooses other road which seemed less traveled. This decision, which seems simple, actually represents important life choices that shape our future.

Frost uses nature imagery to convey his message. The roads are in yellow wood, which suggests autumn season. Autumn is time when things are changing and ending, so this reinforces the idea of transition and change in life. The roads are described as "worn about the same," which means that both choices are equally valid. This is important because it shows that there is no obviously right or wrong choice.

The speaker says he will tell this story "with a sigh" in future. This phrase is ambiguous because sigh can indicate regret or satisfaction. The reader cannot know if speaker is happy or sad about his choice. This ambiguity is central to poem's meaning. It suggests that we cannot know if our choices were right until much later, and even then, we might have mixed feelings.

One of most interesting aspects of poem is the final stanza. The speaker says that taking the road less traveled "has made all the difference." However, earlier in poem, he said both roads were "really about the same." This contradiction suggests that speaker is reconstructing his past, perhaps making his choice seem more significant than it actually was. This is something what people often do when they look back at their lives.

The poem teaches us that life is full of choices and we must make decisions even when we don't have all information. Each choice leads to different path and different outcomes. Also, we tend to romanticize our past choices, believing that they were more meaningful than they actually were at time.

In conclusion, "The Road Not Taken" is not simply about taking less conventional path in life, as many people believe. It is more complex meditation on how we make choices and how we remember them later. Frost's poem reminds us that our life stories are partly created by our memories and interpretations of past, not just by facts of what happened.
