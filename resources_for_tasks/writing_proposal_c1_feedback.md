# Writing Sample: Proposal

**Task**: Write a proposal (250-300 words) for your company suggesting how to improve communication between the engineering and marketing departments

## Student's Proposal

Proposal: Improving Cross-Department Communication Between Engineering and Marketing

1. Background

Over the past year, several product launches have experienced delays attributable in large part to miscommunication between the engineering and marketing departments. The most recent example -- the delayed release of the X200 sensor -- resulted in a revised timeline that costed the company an estimated EUR 45,000 in missed revenue. This proposal outlines measures to address these recurring communication breakdowns.

2. Current Situation

At present, the two departments operate with largely independent workflows and hold joint meetings only on a quarterly base. Project specifications are exchanged primarily through email, which has led to multiple instances where critical technical constraints were not communicated before marketing materials had already been finalised. Additionally, each department uses different project management platforms, meaning that updates from one side are not automatically visible for the other.

3. Proposed Solutions

Firstly, I recommend to establish bi-weekly cross-departmental stand-up meetings of no more than 20 minutes, during which ongoing projects can be briefly reviewed. Secondly, it would be advisable that both departments migrate to a unified project management platform. While it requires an initial investment, the long-term benefits in terms of transparency outweigh clearly the costs. Thirdly, I propose the creation of a liaison role -- a "technical communicator" -- who would be responsible for to translate engineering specifications into language accessible to the marketing team, and vice versa.

4. Conclusion

In summary, these measures would address the root causes of the current communication inefficiencies. I would welcome the opportunity to discuss this proposal further and to present a more detailed implementation timeline to the management board.
