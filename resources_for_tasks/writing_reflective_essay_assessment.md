# Writing Assessment: Reflective Essay

**Student**: B1 secondary student, aged 14-16, Bishkek, Kyrgyzstan
**Task**: Reflective essay about learning experience
**Key Issues**: Reflective language, past tense consistency, expressing personal growth

---

## Student's Essay (350 words)

My English Learning Journey

When I first started learning English three years ago, I was very nervous and not confident. I remember my first English class in grade 7. The teacher spoke only in English and I couldn't understand anything. I feel very lost and confused. Now, looking back, I can see how much I have improved and learned many important lessons.

At the beginning, my biggest challenge was speaking. I was too shy to speak English in front of my classmates because I was afraid to make mistakes. I always think that everyone will laugh at me if I say something wrong. So I usually stay quiet in class even when I know the answer. This was a big problem because I can't practice speaking if I don't talk.

The turning point came last year when my teacher organized a group project. We have to create a presentation about environmental problems. I was in a group with three other students. At first, I wanted to do only the writing part, but my group members encouraged me to speak during the presentation. They helped me practice and told me that making mistakes is normal and important for learning.

When we did the presentation, I was extremely nervous. My hands were shaking and my voice was quiet. But I did it! And actually, nobody laughed. Instead, my classmates asked interesting questions and we had a good discussion. This experience teaches me that I need to be brave and take risks to improve.

After that, I started participating more in class. I still make mistakes, but now I don't worry so much about them. I learned that the most important thing is trying to communicate, not speaking perfectly. My teacher says that fluency is more important than accuracy at my level.

Now I feel much more confident in English. I can understand most of what my teacher says, I can have basic conversations, and I can write simple essays like this one. My goal for the future is to watch English movies without subtitles and maybe study abroad in an English-speaking country someday.

This learning journey taught me not only English but also important life lessons about confidence, perseverance, and not being afraid of failure.

---

**Strengths**: Personal and authentic, shows clear progression over time, appropriate length, good self-awareness
**Areas for Improvement**:
- Past tense consistency: "I feel very lost" (should be "felt"), "I always think" (should be "thought"), "stay quiet" (should be "stayed"), "This experience teaches me" (should be "taught")
- Reflective language: Could use more reflective phrases like "I realized that," "This made me understand," "Looking back, I now see that," "I learned that" (used once but could use more)
- Present perfect vs simple past: Some confusion - "I have improved" (correct), "We have to create" (should be "had to")
- Modal verbs: "I can't practice" (should be "couldn't" in past context)
- Expressing growth: Could be more explicit about what changed - focuses on confidence but could discuss specific language skills gained
- Conclusion: Slightly abrupt - could expand on the "life lessons" mentioned rather than just listing them
