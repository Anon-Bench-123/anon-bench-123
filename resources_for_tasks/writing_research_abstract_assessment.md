## Student's Abstract

**Title**: Exploring the Impact of Task-Based Language Teaching on Oral Fluency Development in Albanian EFL Secondary Classrooms

**Abstract**

Task-based language teaching (TBLT) has been gaining considerable attention in recent years as an effective approach for developing communicative competence in foreign language learners. This study is investigating the effects of TBLT on oral fluency among Albanian secondary school students who are learning English as a foreign language.

The Albanian educational context presents unique challenges for English language instruction. Most secondary schools rely heavily on grammar-translation methods, with limited opportunities for students to engage in authentic communicative activities. Students often achieve high scores on written grammar tests but they struggle to speak fluently and confidently. This gap between grammatical knowledge and communicative ability motivated the current research.

The study employs a quasi-experimental design with 120 participants (ages 15-16) from two secondary schools in Tirana. The experimental group receives TBLT instruction for one academic semester (12 weeks), while the control group receives traditional grammar-focused instruction. Both groups studied the same curriculum topics and vocabulary.

Oral fluency is being measured through pre-tests, post-tests, and delayed post-tests (4 weeks after the intervention) using monologue and dialogue tasks. Fluency measures include speech rate (words per minute), mean length of runs (words between pauses), and phonation-time ratio (speaking time divided by total time). The research also collected data through teacher interviews and student questionnaires to understand perceptions of TBLT.

Preliminary results from the pre- and post-test data suggests that the experimental group showed significantly greater gains in all three fluency measures compared to the control group. Interestingly, the delayed post-test data indicated that these gains were maintained over time. Qualitative data reveals that students in the experimental group reported increased confidence and motivation to speak English.

These findings have important implications for English language teaching in Albania and similar EFL contexts where grammar-focused instruction dominates. The study demonstrates that TBLT can be successfully implemented in contexts with large class sizes and limited resources. Recommendations for teacher training and curriculum development will be discussed.
