# Writing Assessment: Essay on Social Media

**Student**: B2 secondary student, aged 16-18, Ulaanbaatar, Mongolia
**Task**: Opinion essay about social media's impact on society
**Key Issues**: Balanced argumentation, paragraph cohesion, avoiding overgeneralization

---

## Student's Essay (450 words)

The Impact of Social Media on Modern Society

Social media has completely changed our lives in the 21st century. Nowadays, everyone uses platforms like Facebook, Instagram, and Twitter every single day. While some people think social media is great, others believe it's destroying society. In this essay, I will discuss both sides and give my opinion.

First of all, social media has many positive aspects. The most important benefit is that it connects people from all around the world. For example, I have friends in South Korea, Russia, and the United States that I met through Instagram, and we chat regularly even though we never met in person. This kind of international friendship was impossible before social media existed. Additionally, social media is very useful for education. Students can join study groups, watch educational videos, and ask teachers questions online. During the COVID-19 pandemic, social media became even more important because it allowed people to stay connected when they couldn't meet face-to-face.

Another advantage is that social media gives everyone a voice. In the past, only journalists and politicians could share their opinions with large audiences. Now, anyone can post their thoughts and millions of people might read them. This is good for democracy because citizens can participate in discussions about important issues. Social movements like Black Lives Matter became powerful through social media.

On the other hand, social media also has serious negative effects. One major problem is that it causes mental health issues. Studies show that young people who spend a lot of time on social media are more likely to feel depressed and anxious. This is because people always compare themselves to others and feel like their life is not good enough. Everyone posts only their best moments, so social media creates unrealistic expectations.

Furthermore, social media spreads false information very quickly. Anyone can post anything, even if it's not true, and thousands of people will believe it and share it before anyone checks the facts. This is dangerous for society because people make decisions based on lies. During elections, false news on social media can influence how people vote.

Cyberbullying is another serious issue. People say terrible things online that they would never say in person because they can hide behind fake profiles. This causes real harm to victims, especially teenagers.

In conclusion, social media is a powerful tool that has both positive and negative effects. It connects people and provides useful information, but it also causes mental health problems and spreads misinformation. I believe that social media itself is not bad, but people need to use it more responsibly. Schools should teach students about digital citizenship and critical thinking so they can benefit from social media without being harmed by it.

---

**Strengths**: Clear structure with introduction, body paragraphs, and conclusion; attempts to present both sides; relevant examples; appropriate length
**Areas for Improvement**:
- Overgeneralization: "everyone uses platforms...every single day" (too absolute), "everyone posts only their best moments" (sweeping claim), "anyone can post anything" (technically true but lacks nuance)
- Paragraph cohesion: Body paragraphs jump between ideas - second paragraph covers connection AND education AND pandemic - should be split into separate paragraphs with clearer topic sentences
- Balanced argumentation: Negative side has three paragraphs with specific problems (mental health, misinformation, cyberbullying) while positive side is condensed into two paragraphs covering five different benefits - imbalanced development
- Evidence integration: "Studies show that..." without citing specific studies weakens the argument
- Personal examples: Relies too heavily on personal experience ("I have friends...") rather than broader societal analysis expected at B2 level
