# Writing Sample: Argumentative Essay

**Student**: student-1, age 22, Tertiary
**Task**: Write a 300-word argumentative essay on the following topic: "Universities should make sustainability a compulsory part of every degree programme." To what extent do you agree or disagree?

---

## Student's Essay

The Role of Sustainability in Higher Education

In recent years, sustainability has become a crucial global concern, and universities play a significant role in shaping the future professionals who will address these challenges. I strongly agree that sustainability should be a compulsory part of every degree programme, and in this essay I will outline my reasons.

Firstly, it is undeniable that environmental issues affect all sectors of society. Whether a student is studying engineering, business, medicine or the arts, they will encounter sustainability-related challenges in their career. For example, an architect need to understand sustainable building materials, while a business graduate must be familiar with corporate social responsibility frameworks. Making sustainability compulsory ensures that all graduates, regardless their field, possess a basic understanding of these critical issues.

Secondly, universities have a responsibility to promote critical thinking about global challenges. By integrating sustainability into core curricula, institutions encourage students not only to understand the problems but also to develop innovative solutions. Research has demonstrated that interdisciplinary approaches to sustainability education leads to more creative problem-solving. Furthermore, students who are exposed to sustainability concepts during their studies are more likely to implement sustainable practices in their professional lifes.

However, some may argue that adding compulsory modules could overload already demanding degree programmes. While this concern is valid, the issue can be addressed by integrating sustainability themes into existing modules rather than create entirely new courses. This approach would be less disruptive and could actually enrich the existing curriculum by providing real-world context to theoretical knowledge.

In conclusion, the integration of sustainability education across all degree programmes is not merely desirable but essential. As universities are responsible to prepare students for the challenges of the modern world, sustainability must be in the core of this preparation. The benefits of producing environmentally-conscious graduates far outweigh the logistical challenges of curriculum reform.
